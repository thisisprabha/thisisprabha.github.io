About Sip up!

Sip up! is a beautiful and intuitive water intake tracking app designed for iOS and Apple Watch. Stay hydrated with stunning water wave animations, smart reminders, and comprehensive health integration.

Key Features

  • Beautiful Water Wave UI - Physics-based animations that respond to device motion
  • iOS Home Screen Widget - Track your progress at a glance with our interactive widget
  • Apple Watch App - Full-featured watch app with gesture controls and complications
  • HealthKit Integration - Automatically syncs with Apple Health for comprehensive tracking
  • Smart Notifications - Personalized reminders based on your motivation type (9 AM - 6 PM)
  • Streak Tracking - Build and maintain your hydration streak
  • Yearly Analytics - Visualize your progress over time
  • Customizable Settings - Set your daily goal, water per glass/bottle, and motivation type
  • Real-time Sync - Seamless synchronization between iPhone and Apple Watch

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I set up the app?
  1. Open the app and grant HealthKit permissions when prompted
  2. Go to Settings to customize your daily goal and water per glass/bottle
  3. Choose your motivation type (Kidney Health, Weight Loss, or Energy Boost)
  4. Enable notifications if you want smart reminders
  5. Start tracking by tapping the "+" button or using gestures on Apple Watch
How do I add the iOS widget to my home screen?
  1. Long press on your home screen to enter edit mode
  2. Tap the "+" button in the top-left corner
  3. Search for "Sip up!" or "Water Intake"
  4. Select the widget size (Small or Medium)
  5. Tap "Add Widget" and position it on your home screen
  6. You can tap the "+" button on the widget to quickly add water!
How does the Apple Watch app work?
The Apple Watch app offers intuitive gesture controls:
  • Swipe Right/Up: Add one glass/bottle of water
  • Swipe Left: Remove the last entry
  • Double Tap: View your daily summary
The app also supports watch face complications so you can see your progress at a glance!
Why isn't my data syncing between iPhone and Apple Watch?
The app uses multiple sync methods for reliability:
  1. Ensure both apps are installed and have been opened at least once
  2. Check that both devices are connected (same Apple ID, Bluetooth enabled)
  3. Restart both apps if sync seems delayed
  4. Verify HealthKit permissions are granted on both devices
  5. If issues persist, restart both devices
The app automatically uses HealthKit as the source of truth, so data should sync through Apple Health.
How are notifications scheduled?
Smart notifications are sent between 9 AM and 6 PM only:
  • Notifications are sent 1 hour after your last water intake (if goal not reached)
  • No notifications are sent if you've reached your daily goal
  • Notifications stop after 6 PM to avoid disturbing your evening
  • Messages are personalized based on your selected motivation type
What motivation types are available?
Choose from three motivation types in Settings:
  • Kidney Health: Focuses on kidney function and overall health
  • Weight Loss: Emphasizes metabolism and weight management benefits
  • Energy Boost: Highlights energy levels and mental clarity
Your choice affects the notification messages you receive.
How is my streak calculated?
Your streak is calculated based on consecutive days where you've met your daily goal:
  • The streak counts days where you drank at least your daily goal amount
  • If you miss a day, your streak resets to 0
  • Data is pulled from HealthKit, so it's accurate and reliable
  • The streak updates automatically as you log water throughout the day
Can I use the app without HealthKit?
HealthKit integration is recommended for the best experience, as it:
  • Ensures accurate data synchronization
  • Allows data to sync across all your Apple devices
  • Provides reliable streak calculations
  • Integrates with other health apps
However, the app will still function with local storage if HealthKit is not available.

Troubleshooting

Widget Not Appearing

Step 1: Ensure you've built and run the main app at least once before adding the widget
Step 2: Check that the widget extension is properly installed (it installs with the main app)
Step 3: Try restarting your device if the widget still doesn't appear

HealthKit Authorization Issues

Step 1: Go to Settings > Privacy & Security > Health > Sip up!
Step 2: Ensure "Water" is enabled for both "Read Data" and "Write Data"
Step 3: If permissions were denied, you may need to reinstall the app to see the permission prompt again

Watch App Not Syncing

Step 1: Ensure both iPhone and Apple Watch are connected and on the same Apple ID
Step 2: Open both apps at least once to establish the connection
Step 3: Check that HealthKit permissions are granted on both devices
Step 4: Try force-quitting and reopening both apps

Notifications Not Working

Step 1: Go to Settings > Notifications > Sip up! and ensure notifications are enabled
Step 2: Check that you haven't reached your daily goal (notifications stop when goal is met)
Step 3: Verify the current time is between 9 AM and 6 PM (notifications only work during these hours)
Step 4: Ensure Do Not Disturb or Focus modes aren't blocking notifications

System Requirements

  • iOS: iOS 16.0 or later
  • watchOS: watchOS 8.0 or later (for Apple Watch app)
  • Device: iPhone and/or Apple Watch
  • Permissions: HealthKit access (recommended), Motion & Fitness (for watch gestures), Notifications (optional)

Privacy & Data

Your privacy is important to us. Sip up! uses:

  • HealthKit: To read and write water intake data. This data stays on your device and in your iCloud account (if enabled).
  • App Group: For syncing data between iPhone and Apple Watch apps. Data is stored locally on your devices.
  • No Third-Party Services: We don't collect, store, or transmit your data to any external servers.

All data is stored locally on your device and synced through Apple's HealthKit and iCloud services. We have no access to your personal health data.
